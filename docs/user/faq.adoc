== General Questions

=== What is ExamCraft AI?

ExamCraft AI is an intelligent platform that helps educators automatically generate high-quality exam questions using artificial intelligence. It reads your course materials and creates contextual, pedagogically sound questions in minutes instead of hours.

=== How does it work?

. **Upload** your course documents (PDFs, Word files, etc.)
. **Select** topics you want questions about
. **Choose** difficulty level and question types
. **Generate** questions using AI based on your content
. **Review** and edit questions as needed
. **Export** to your preferred format

=== What file formats are supported?

* **PDF** - Text-based PDFs (not scanned images)
* **Word Documents** - DOC and DOCX files
* **Text Files** - Plain text (.txt)
* **Markdown** - Structured markdown files (.md)

Maximum file size: 50 MB per document.

=== How accurate are the generated questions?

Questions are generated directly from your uploaded content, ensuring high accuracy and relevance. The AI doesn't invent information - it creates questions based on what you've provided. Additionally, you review every question before use, maintaining complete quality control.

== Usage Questions

=== How many questions can I generate?

There are no hard limits on question generation. Practical limits depend on:

* Document content quality and length
* Your subscription plan
* AI service capacity

Typical usage: 10-50 questions per document, hundreds per course.

=== Can I edit generated questions?

Yes! You have complete editorial control:

* **Modify question text** and wording
* **Change answer options** for multiple choice
* **Adjust difficulty level** assignments
* **Add or edit explanations**
* **Approve or reject** any question

=== What question types are available?

**Currently available**:
* Multiple choice (4 options)
* Open-ended questions

**Coming soon**:
* True/False with explanations
* Fill-in-the-blank
* Matching exercises
* Code completion (for programming courses)

=== How do I control question difficulty?

Questions are generated at three levels:

* **Easy**: Basic recall and recognition
* **Medium**: Application and analysis
* **Hard**: Synthesis and evaluation

The AI uses Bloom's Taxonomy principles to ensure appropriate cognitive levels.

== Troubleshooting

=== Document upload fails

**Common causes and solutions**:

. **File too large**: Reduce file size or split large documents
. **Unsupported format**: Convert to PDF or Word format
. **Poor internet**: Check connection stability
. **Browser issues**: Clear cache or try different browser

**Steps to resolve**:
[source]
----
1. Check file size (max 50 MB)
2. Verify file format is supported
3. Try uploading a smaller test file
4. Contact support if issue persists
----

=== Questions aren't relevant to my content

**Possible causes**:
* Document content is unclear or poorly structured
* Topic selection is too broad or vague
* Document contains mostly images or tables

**Solutions**:
* Provide clearer, more detailed topic descriptions
* Upload better-structured documents
* Use documents with substantial text content
* Try different sections of your materials

=== Application is slow or unresponsive

**Immediate steps**:
. Refresh your browser page
. Clear browser cache and cookies
. Check internet connection speed
. Try a different browser

**If problems persist**:
* Contact support with details about your experience
* Include browser type and version
* Describe specific actions that are slow

=== Generated questions have errors

**Quality control steps**:

. Review the source content for accuracy
. Check if the topic description was clear
. Verify the AI understood the context correctly
. Edit questions to fix any issues

**Remember**: You approve every question before use!

== Best Practices

=== Preparing documents for best results

**Document quality tips**:

* Use clear, well-structured content
* Include proper headings and sections
* Ensure text is not embedded in images
* Provide complete explanations and examples
* Remove irrelevant appendices or references

**Content organization**:

* One main topic per document works best
* Include learning objectives when possible
* Provide clear definitions of key terms
* Use consistent terminology throughout

=== Writing effective topic descriptions

**Good topic descriptions**:

* "Python variable types and scope rules"
* "Marketing mix: product, price, place, promotion"
* "Causes and consequences of World War I"

**Avoid vague descriptions**:

* "Python" (too broad)
* "Marketing" (too general)
* "History" (not specific enough)

=== Reviewing generated questions

**Quality checklist**:

* ✅ Question is clear and unambiguous
* ✅ Correct answer is indisputable
* ✅ Wrong answers are plausible but clearly incorrect
* ✅ Language level is appropriate for students
* ✅ Content aligns with learning objectives

=== Managing your question library

**Organization tips**:

* Use descriptive tags and categories
* Mark questions by difficulty and topic
* Track which questions have been used
* Regular review and updates
* Backup important question sets
